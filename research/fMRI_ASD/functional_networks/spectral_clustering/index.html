def compute():
    L = nx.laplacian_matrix(G).toarray()
    vals, vecs = np.linalg.eig(L)
    plt.title("Eigen value distribution")
    plt.plot(vals)
    plt.show()
    print(vals)
    print("No. of zeros in eigenvalue = {0}".format((vals == 0).sum()))

Why Graph Laplacian is important¶

No. of zero eigenvalue of Laplacian Matrix correspond to number of connected component in the graph. Lets add edges to see what become of eigenvalue spectrum.

Let this be the final form of the graph. Now, we will understand how to partition this graph based on its laplacian's eigenvalue spectrum (hence the name, Spectral Clustering Algorithm.) We will use Fiedler’s Theory of Spectral Graph Partitioning.

vals, vecs = np.linalg.eig(L)
In [18]:
from sklearn.cluster import KMeans
vecs = vecs[:,np.argsort(vals)]
vals = vals[np.argsort(vals)]

def cluster(k):
    kmeans = KMeans(n_clusters=k)
    kmeans.fit(vecs[:,1:k])
    colors = kmeans.labels_
    nx.draw(G, with_labels=1, node_color=colors)
